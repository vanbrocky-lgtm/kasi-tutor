WHAT IS AN INCOME STATEMENT?

Think of it like a report card for a business. It answers one question:
Did the business make a profit or a loss?
🧠 BIG IDEA (VERY IMPORTANT)
There are only 2 types of accounts:

IncomeExpenses
Money coming INTO the businessMoney going OUT of the business

STRUCTURE (HOW IT IS BUILT) We calculate in this order:

  1. >Sales
  2. Cost of Sales
  3. Get Gross Profit
  4. Add Other Income
  5. Subtract Expenses
  6. Get Net Profit
1. SALES (Income )
This is the main income of the business
Comes from selling goods
Example:
The business sells goods for R20 000
Sales = R20 000

2. COST OF SALES(Expense )
This is what it costs to buy the goods you sold
Formula:
Opening Stock + Purchases – Closing Stock
Example:
Opening stock = R5 000
Purchases = R15 000
Closing stock = R4 000
Calculation: 5 000 + 15 000 – 4 000 = R16 000
    
3.GROSS PROFIT
Profit made only from trading
Formula:
Sales – Cost of Sales
Example:
20 000 – 16 000 = R4 000
This tells us: ✔️ The business is making money from selling goods
    
4.OTHER INCOME (Income )
Extra income (not main business)
Examples:
Rent income
Commission
Example:
Rent received = R2 000
    
5. EXPENSES (VERY IMPORTANT SECTION )
These are costs to run the business
-Salaries & Wages (Expense )
Paying workers
Example: R8 000
    
-Rent Expense (Expense )
Paying for the building
Example: R3 000
    
-Water & Electricity (Expense )
Utilities
Example: R1 200
    
-Telephone (Expense )
Communication
Example: R500
    
Advertising (Expense )
Marketing the business
Example: R1 000
    
- Insurance (Expense )
Protection of assets
Example: R800
    
-Depreciation (Expense )
Assets losing value over time
Example:
Asset = R10 000
Rate = 10%
 10% × 10 000 = R1 000
    
- Bad Debts (Expense )
Customers who didn’t pay
Example: R600
    
- Trading Stock Deficit (Expense )
Missing stock
Example: R400
    
6.Accrued expense vs Accrued income vs Prepaid expense vs Income received in advance
    
1. ACCRUED EXPENSES (Expense owing)
 Definition
=An expense that belongs to this year but has NOT been paid yet
=The business owes money
-Example
Water & electricity for February = R1 000
But it will only be paid in March
#Treatment
It is still an expense for this year 
Add it to expenses
Show as a liability (owing)
+Calculation
Expense = 5 000 + 1 000 = R6 000
?How to think:
“We used it, so we must record it”
    
2. ACCRUED INCOME (Income owed to us)
 Definition
=Income that belongs to this year but has NOT been received yet
=Someone owes the business money
-Example
Rent income earned = R2 000
But not yet received
#Treatment
It is still income 
Add it to income
Show as an asset (owing to us)
+Calculation
Income = 8 000 + 2 000 = R10 000
?How to think:
“We earned it, even if we didn’t get cash”
    
3. PREPAID EXPENSE (Expense paid early)
 Definition
= Expense that was paid in advance, but part belongs to next year
- Example
Insurance paid = R2 400 for 12 months
Only 6 months used this year
# Treatment
Only part is an expense 
The rest is an asset (prepaid)
+ Calculation
Monthly = 2 400 ÷ 12 = 200
Used = 200 × 6 = R1 200
Expense = R1 200
Prepaid = R1 200
? How to think:
“We paid too much — some is for next year”
    
4. INCOME RECEIVED IN ADVANCE (Unearned income)
 Definition
= Money received before it is earned
- Example
Received rent = R3 000
But R1 000 is for next year
# Treatment
Only part is income 
The rest is a liability (we owe service)
+ Calculation
Income = 3 000 – 1 000 = R2 000
? How to think:
“We got cash, but we haven’t earned all
